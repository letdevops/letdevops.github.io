Fee schedule. Our fee is 50% of the transaction amount.

Refunds and exceptional charges. 3A. SYSTEM DEVELOPERS: YOUR OBLIGATION NOT TO SOLICIT CUSTOMERS AWAY FROM ItPost

ItPost spends a large amount of money marketing to consumers and attracting them to the site, where they can subscribe to trading strategies like yours and thus pay you money. When they pay you, ItPost charges you a fee for our service. From our perspective, ItPost invests a large amount of money up front each time we attract a new customer to your trading strategy, which we hope to make back (and eventually a profit) over the customer’s lifetime.

There is a natural temptation for strategy vendors to use ItPost to find new customers (and to allow ItPost to bear the cost of customer acquisition), but then to “transition” the customer to an alternate venue where he can pay you directly, without your incurring ItPost fees. We prohibit this. What does this mean, concretely? First, you are welcome to offer your trading system on sites other than ItPost. We also welcome you to run your own Web site independently of ItPost. That said, if you pursue either of those strategies, we ask you not to overtly or implicitly solicit ItPost customers and ask them to move their subscriptions to alternative platforms, where subscriptions or payments would be processed outside of ItPost’s control. Second, if you do offer your strategy elsewhere, you may not offer it for a lower price at another venue (or, alternatively, you may not tack on a fee for ItPost subscribers). Third, while you are welcome to sell your system from as many Web sites or through as many channels as you like, we ask that you do not advertise those other channels from the ItPost Web site. That means, if you run a Web site independent of ItPost, and your Web site allows subscriptions or offers services outside of ItPost (such as “a managed account”) you may not include a link to that Web site, or implicitly advertise that Web site anywhere on ItPost (including your system description on ItPost, your forums on ItPost, or public/private messages to ItPost subscribers). Any of these activities are either subtle or overt ways to encourage customers found for you by ItPost to migrate away from ItPost.

The purpose of these rules is to allow you to market to customers in as many places as you like, and to give you freedom to to reach as many customers as you possibly can… but to require that you be fair and not “poach” customers acquired by ItPost on your behalf. If you have any questions about this, please contact us by email so that we can avoid misunderstandings and conflict. Our goal is to make ItPost so great that you will have little desire to offer your trading strategy elsewhere. But, should you desire to offer your strategy elsewhere, we will certainly allow this, providing the playing field is fair.

If you violate any of these provisions, we may temporarily or permanently suspend your account, prevent your system from being viewed by the public, or take whatever steps we deem necessary to protect the viability of ItPost. We will not issue any refunds – in whole or in part – if we suspend your system for violation of these Terms of Service.
